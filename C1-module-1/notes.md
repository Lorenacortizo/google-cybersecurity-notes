# Module 1 - Cybersecurity Fundamentals

## 📌 Key Terms and Definitions

### 🔐 Cybersecurity
The practise of ensuring **confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA)** of information by protecting networks, devices, people, and data from unathorized access or criminal explotation

---

### ☁️ Cloud Security
The process of ensuring that cloud-based resources are properly configured and access is restricted to users only

---

### 👤 Insider Threat
A current or former employee, contractor, or trusted partner who poses a security risk

---

### 🌐 Network Security
The practise of protecting an organization's network infrastucture from unathorized accces

---

### 🆔 Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
Any information that can be used to identify an individual

---

### 🔒 Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)
A specific type of PII that requires stricter handling and protection

---

### 🛡️ Security Posture
An organization's ability to manage and defend its critical assets and data, and respond to changes or threats

---

### ⚠️ Threat
Any circumstance or event that could negatively impact systems, data, or resources

---

### 👥 Threat Actor
Any individual or group that poses a potencial risk to security

---

### 🧠 Technical Skills
Skills that involve knowledge of spetific tools, procedures, and security policies

---

### 🔄 Transferable Skills
Skills gained in other areas that can be applied across different roles or careers

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways
- Cybersecurity is based on protecting data and systems using the **CIA triad**
- NOt all threats come from outside - insider threats are important
- Protecting personal data (PII/SPII) is critical
- Both technical and transferable skills are valueble in cybersecurity

---
