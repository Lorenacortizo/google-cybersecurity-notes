# 🛡️ Module 3 – Terms and Definitions

## 🧠 Core Security Concepts

### Resource
A **resource** is anything that has value to an organization.  
This can include data, systems, applications, or infrastructure.

---

### Availability
Availability means that **authorized users can access data when needed**.  
It ensures systems and information remain accessible and operational.

---

### Confidentiality
Confidentiality ensures that **only authorized individuals can access specific data or resources**.  
It focuses on preventing unauthorized disclosure.

---

### Integrity
Integrity means that **data is accurate, authentic, and trustworthy**.  
It protects information from being altered or tampered with.

---

## 🔺 CIA Triad

### CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)
The **CIA Triad** is a fundamental model in cybersecurity used to guide security decisions.

- **Confidentiality** → restrict access  
- **Integrity** → ensure data accuracy  
- **Availability** → ensure access when needed  

It helps organizations evaluate and manage risk when designing systems and policies.

---

## ⚖️ Compliance & Privacy

### Compliance
Compliance is the process of **following internal policies and external regulations**.

---

### Privacy Protection
Privacy protection involves **safeguarding personal data from unauthorized use**.

---

### PHI (Protected Health Information)
PHI refers to **health-related data about an individual**, including:
- medical history  
- current conditions  
- future health information  

---

### HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
A U.S. law designed to **protect sensitive health information (PHI)**.

---

### SPII (Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information)
A more critical type of personal data that requires **stricter handling**, such as:
- Social Security numbers  
- financial data  
- biometric data  

---

## 🏗️ Security Structure

### Security Architecture
A **security architecture** is the overall design of security systems, including:
- tools  
- processes  
- policies  

It is used to protect against risks and threats.

---

### Security Controls
Security controls are **measures put in place to reduce specific risks**.

Examples:
- firewalls  
- access controls  
- encryption  

---

### Security Frameworks
Security frameworks provide **structured guidelines** to manage risks and protect data.

---

### NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
The **NIST CSF** is a widely used voluntary framework that includes:
- standards  
- guidelines  
- best practices  

It helps organizations manage cybersecurity risk effectively.

---

### Security Governance
Security governance refers to **how an organization manages and directs its security efforts**.

It includes:
- policies  
- leadership decisions  
- risk management strategies  

---

## 🧑‍💻 Ethics & Threat Actors

### Security Ethics
Security ethics are **guidelines for making responsible decisions** as a security professional.

---

### Hacktivist
A hacktivist is someone who uses hacking techniques to **promote political or social causes**.

---
