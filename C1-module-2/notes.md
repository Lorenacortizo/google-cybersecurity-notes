# Module 2 – Cybersecurity Attacks & Threats

## 📌 Key Terms and Definitions

### 🤖 Adversarial Artificial Intelligence (AI)
A technique that manipulates AI and machine learning systems to perform attacks more effectively.

---

### 📧 Business Email Compromise (BEC)
A type of phishing attack where a threat actor impersonates a trusted source to gain financial advantage.

---

### 🎓 CISSP (Certified Information Systems Security Professional)
A globally recognized cybersecurity certification awarded by (ISC)², demonstrating expertise in information security.

---

### 🦠 Computer Virus
Malicious code designed to interfere with computer operations and damage data or software.

---

### 🔐 Cryptographic Attack
An attack that targets secure communication methods between sender and receiver.

---

### 💻 Hacker
An individual who uses computers to access systems, networks, or data (can be ethical or malicious).

---

### 🛑 Malware (Malicious Software)
Software specifically designed to harm devices, networks, or data.

---

### 🔑 Password Cracking Attack
An attempt to gain unauthorized access to systems or data by breaking or guessing passwords.

---

### 🎣 Phishing
The use of digital communications to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information or installing malware.

---

### 🏢 Physical Attack
A security incident that affects physical environments as well as digital systems.

---

### 🚪 Physical Social Engineering
An attack where a threat actor impersonates someone (employee, vendor, etc.) to gain physical access to a location.

---

### 🧠 Social Engineering
A manipulation technique that exploits human error to gain private information, access, or valuables.

---

### 📱 Social Media Phishing
An attack where the threat actor gathers detailed information from social media before targeting the victim.

---

### 🎯 Spear Phishing
A targeted phishing attack directed at a specific individual or group, appearing to come from a trusted source.

---

### 🔗 Supply Chain Attack
An attack targeting systems, software, or hardware by exploiting vulnerabilities in the supply chain.

---

### 💾 USB Baiting
An attack where a malicious USB device is left for someone to find and use, infecting systems unknowingly.

---

### 📞 Vishing (Voice Phishing)
The use of voice communication (calls, VoIP) to trick individuals into revealing confidential information.

---

### 🌊 Watering Hole Attack
An attack where a frequently visited website is compromised to infect a specific group of users.

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways
- Many cyberattacks exploit **human behavior**, not just technical vulnerabilities  
- Phishing has multiple forms (BEC, spear phishing, vishing, social media phishing)  
- Malware can be delivered in different ways (USB, websites, email)  
- Attacks can target both **digital and physical environments**  

---

## 📈 What I Learned
- Social engineering is one of the most common attack methods  
- Attackers often rely on trust and human mistakes  
- Understanding attack types is essential for prevention and defense
