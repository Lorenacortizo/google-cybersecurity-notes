# 📘 Module 1: Key Terms & Definitions (Course 2)

This module introduces core concepts related to risk management, threats, and the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF).

---

## 🛡️ NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)

- **Prepare**  
  The first step of the RMF. Involves activities needed to manage security and privacy risks before a breach occurs.

- **Categorize**  
  The second step. Used to classify systems and data to support risk management processes.

- **Select**  
  The third step. Involves choosing and documenting appropriate security controls.

- **Implement**  
  The fourth step. Refers to applying security and privacy controls within the organization.

- **Assess (Evaluate)**  
  The fifth step. Determines whether controls are properly implemented and effective.

- **Authorize**  
  The sixth step. Assigns responsibility for security and privacy risks within the organization.

- **Monitor**  
  The seventh step. Involves continuously tracking system performance and security posture.

---

## ⚠️ Threats & Risks

- **Risk**  
  Anything that can impact the confidentiality, integrity, or availability (CIA) of an asset.

- **Risk Mitigation**  
  The process of reducing the impact of risks through policies, procedures, and controls.

- **Vulnerability**  
  A weakness that can be exploited by a threat.

- **External Threat**  
  Any threat originating outside the organization.

- **Internal Threat**  
  A risk posed by current or former employees, vendors, or trusted partners.

---

## 💻 Attack Types

- **Ransomware**  
  A type of malware that encrypts data and demands payment to restore access.

- **Social Engineering**  
  A manipulation technique that exploits human error to gain access to sensitive information or systems.

---

## 🏢 Organizational Security

- **Business Continuity**  
  The ability of an organization to maintain operations during and after a disruption.

- **Security Posture**  
  An organization’s overall ability to protect assets and respond to security incidents.

- **Shared Responsibility**  
  The concept that all individuals in an organization play a role in maintaining security.

---

## 🎯 Key Takeaways

- The NIST RMF provides a structured approach to managing risk.  
- Understanding threats, vulnerabilities, and risks is essential for defense.  
- Security is a shared responsibility across the organization.  
- Continuous monitoring is key to maintaining a strong security posture.
